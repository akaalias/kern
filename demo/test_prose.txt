Come and learn what I've learned! I haven't posted my retrospectives in a while. Last week was the first week that I had fully to myself and I challenged myself to establish a solid deep work routine at home. Here are some of the things that worked, that were okay and some that didn't work.

# Retrospective Week 13 - A total of 13 hours completed.

## What worked:

- I got 13 hours of fully concentrated work in. Awesome! I built myself a custom Scoreboard, too, to keep track of each day's work-hours.
- I felt productive.
- Started and finished a fully functional Minimum Viable Product called "RedditGrowth" which automatically finds the best subreddits and times to post content for maximum engagement.

## What didn't work:

- I still struggled with the morning routine. Getting started before 9am seems nearly impossible some days.
- Context switching between projects remains a productivity killer.

## Lessons learned:

Deep work isn't just about time management. It's about energy management. The quality of your focus matters far more than the quantity of hours you put in. A single hour of truly deep, undistracted work can produce more meaningful output than an entire afternoon of shallow multitasking.

The key insight from this week: rituals matter. Having a consistent startup ritual - making coffee, closing all browser tabs, putting on the same playlist - signals to your brain that it's time to enter deep work mode. It's like a warm-up routine for your mind.

Another thing I noticed: the environment shapes behavior more than willpower does. When I moved my phone to another room, I didn't need willpower to avoid checking it. When I blocked social media at the router level, I didn't need discipline to stay focused. Design your environment for success, and discipline becomes almost unnecessary.
